London's National Gallery

 

 

Description

The National Gallery is an art museum in Trafalgar Square, in the centre of London. Founded in 1824, it houses a collection of more than 2,300 paintings dating from the 13th to the early 20th century.

History

The National Gallery was founded in 1824 after the British government bought 38 paintings belonging to the banker John Julius Angerstein. The collection has grown over the years with various donations and acquisitions.
