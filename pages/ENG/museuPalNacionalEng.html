National Palace Museum of Taiwan

 

 

Description

The National Palace Museum of Taiwan houses one of the largest collections of Chinese artifacts and artworks in the world, spanning over 8,000 years of Chinese history from the Neolithic dynasty to the end of the Qing dynasty.

History

The National Palace Museum was founded in 1925 in the Forbidden City in Beijing shortly after the abdication of the last emperor of China. During the Chinese civil war, a large part of the collection was transferred to Taiwan for protection, where it was established in Taipei in 1965.
