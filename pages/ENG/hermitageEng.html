Hermitage Museum

 

 

Description

The Hermitage Museum in St Petersburg, Russia, is one of the largest and oldest museums of art and culture in the world. It was founded in 1764 by Catherine the Great and has been open to the public since 1852.

History

The Hermitage was originally founded as a private museum for Catherine the Great. The collection has grown enormously over the centuries, including important collections of European art, archaeological artefacts and a wide range of pieces from different periods and regions.
