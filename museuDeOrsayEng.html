Orsay Museum

 

 

Description

The Orsay Museum is an art museum located on the left bank of the Seine River in Paris, France. It houses the world's largest collection of impressionist and post-impressionist masterpieces, including works by Monet, Manet, Degas, Renoir, Van Gogh, and others.

History

Opened in 1986, the Orsay Museum is housed in the former Gare d'Orsay railway station, built between 1898 and 1900. The building is a remarkable example of Beaux-Arts architecture and was adapted to function as a museum, preserving its original structure.
